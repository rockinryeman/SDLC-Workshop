### Act 1 — Why product development is slow (Slides 1–6)

**1. Title**
*AI-Powered Product Development* — Building Better Automotive Products with Smaller, Faster,
Cross-Functional Teams.

**2. Why we're here**
A tired driver drifts toward the lane line. Could the car notice — and help? Today, building
that feature takes many teams and many months.

**3. Workshop objectives**
Smaller teams · Faster iteration · Better traceability · Outcome-focused delivery.

**4. ▶ Mentimeter Poll #1**
*Where does your organization spend the most engineering effort?*

**5. Automotive development today**
Specialized functions create handoffs, delays, and rework.

**6. The cost of handoffs**
Requirements → Systems → Architecture → Development → Test → Release.
*(Visual: the handoff chain.)*

### Act 2 — What AI changes (Slides 7–10)

**7. ▶ Mentimeter Poll #2 (Word cloud)**
*What slows product delivery the most today?*

**8. Why specialization exists**
Risk reduction, compliance, deep expertise — good reasons that no longer scale alone.

**9. AI as capability amplification**
AI makes expertise available at the point of need — it augments specialists, not replaces them.

**10. Future-state team**
Product owner + engineers + AI copilot. *(Visual: small cross-functional team.)*

### Act 3 — Live DMS exercise (Slides 11–19)

**11. Our scenario: Driver Monitoring System**
Detect drowsy / distracted / medical-emergency states — and respond safely.

**12. Customer need**
Protect a tired or distracted driver — without constant false alarms.

**13. Step 1 — Requirements**
Generate stakeholder needs, requirements, constraints, acceptance criteria. *(Run Prompt 1.)*

**14. Step 2 — Architecture**
Sensors → AI perception → decision logic → HMI + vehicle interface. *(Run Prompt 2.)*

**15. Step 3 — Model (SysML v2)**
A structured, readable design that traces to requirements. *(Run Prompt 3.)*

**16. Step 4 — Tests & traceability**
Functional, edge, and negative tests + requirements-to-test matrix. *(Run Prompt 4.)*

**17. ▶ Mentimeter Poll #3 (Midpoint)**
*Which SDLC activity benefited most from AI so far?*

**18. Step 5–6 — Implementation & verification**
Detect-assess-respond logic, then a pass/fail review. *(Run Prompts 5–6.)*

**19. Step 7 — Deployment**
OTA rollout, shadow mode, monitoring, rollback. *(Run Prompt 7.)*

### Act 4 — What just happened (Slides 20–22)

**20. The digital thread**
Need → Requirement → Design → Test → Code → Deploy — one connected, auditable trail.

**21. Traditional vs. AI-enabled timeline**
Months of coordination and handoffs → hours with one small team. *(Visual: timeline compare.)*

**22. Where humans stayed in the loop**
Confidence thresholds, safety aggressiveness, bias checks, accountability. AI assisted; people decided.

### Act 5 — Future-state organization (Slides 23–26)

**23. ▶ Mentimeter Poll #4 (Ranking)**

**24. Future operating model**

**25. Key takeaways**
Smaller teams. Faster learning. Better outcomes. Humans remain accountable.

**26. ▶ Mentimeter Poll #5 (Closing) + Q&A**
