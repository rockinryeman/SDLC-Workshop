# Discussion Prompts — DMS Workshop

Five quick **interaction moments** to keep the room engaged — run as a **show of hands**, a
**round-robin**, or an **open call-out**. No tools required; the facilitator captures answers on
a whiteboard or just reacts live.

> Tip: react to the room's answers out loud and reuse their words later in the session — it makes
> the workshop feel co-created.

---

## Moment 1 — Opening (show of hands)
**Where does your organization spend the most engineering effort?**
Requirements · Architecture · Development · Testing · Compliance & Documentation

*Use to:* warm up the room and set up the "handoffs are expensive" point.
*Place at:* ~5 min (during "Why development is slow").

## Moment 2 — What slows us down (round-robin call-out)
**What slows product delivery the most today?**
*(Collect words aloud — "handoffs", "approvals", "requirements churn" — and jot them on a board.)*

*Use to:* generate the room's own language for the problem, then react live.
*Place at:* ~15 min (during "AI and team transformation").

## Moment 3 — Midpoint (quick poll by hands)
**Which SDLC activity benefited most from AI so far?**
Requirements · Architecture · Testing · Implementation · Deployment

*Use to:* break up the 35-min live exercise and keep energy up.
*Place at:* ~45 min (mid live-exercise, after Step 4 or 5).

## Moment 4 — Where to pilot (rank by discussion)
**Where would you pilot AI first?**
Systems Engineering · Software Development · Test Engineering · Program Management · Operations

*Use to:* move the room from "interesting" to "where would we actually start?"
*Place at:* ~75 min (during "Future-state team design").

## Moment 5 — Closing (call-out)
**What is the biggest opportunity?**
Smaller teams · Faster delivery · Better quality · Lower cost · More innovation

*Use to:* end on forward energy and capture the room's priority.
*Place at:* ~88 min (closing).

---

## Optional DMS-specific discussion sparks
- **Trust:** *"How comfortable are you with a car judging whether YOU are too tired to drive?"* —
  great right before the live exercise to surface the ethics/trust angle.
- **False alarms:** *"What's worse — a DMS that occasionally nags you for no reason, or one that
  occasionally misses real drowsiness?"* — sparks the precision-vs-safety debate.
